About DiabetesAI

Understanding the model, dataset, methodology, and limitations of this system.

What is DiabetesAI?

DiabetesAI is an AI-powered diabetes risk screening tool that uses a Gradient Boosting machine learning model trained on 100,000 patient health records. It analyses 8 clinical biomarkers to generate a diabetes risk probability, classifying patients into Low, Medium, or High risk categories.

The system provides feature impact visualisation, clinical range interpretation, a what-if simulator, and a downloadable PDF report — designed to support, not replace, clinical decision-making.

Dataset

The model was trained on a diabetes prediction dataset containing 100,000 patient records from electronic health records. Each record includes 8 clinical features and a binary diabetes diagnosis label.

  • 📊
    100,000 records — 8,500 diabetic (8.5%) and 91,500 non-diabetic (91.5%). Handled via stratified splitting to ensure proportional class representation in both sets.
  • 🔀
    80/20 split — 80,000 training records, 20,000 held out for evaluation. Stratification preserves class ratios in both splits.
  • 🔄
    5-Fold Cross-Validation — Performed across the full dataset to ensure the model generalises well and does not overfit. Mean CV accuracy: 97.18% ± 0.06%.

Methodology

  • 1
    Feature Engineering — Gender and smoking history are label-encoded. All 8 features are passed directly to the model; tree-based methods are invariant to monotonic transformations so no scaling is needed.
  • 2
    Gradient Boosting — 200 decision trees are built sequentially, each correcting errors of the previous. Learning rate (0.08) and subsample (0.8) prevent overfitting.
  • 3
    Probability Output — The model returns a calibrated probability via predict_proba, enabling the 3-tier risk classification (Low <30%, Medium 30–60%, High >60%).
  • 4
    Evaluation — Assessed on test accuracy (97.17%), ROC-AUC (0.979), Precision-Recall AUC (0.884), confusion matrix, and per-class F1 scores.

Medical Disclaimer

DiabetesAI is an informational screening tool only and does not constitute medical advice, diagnosis, or treatment. Predictions are based on statistical patterns and may not apply to every individual. Always consult a licensed physician before making any medical decisions.

If you are experiencing symptoms of diabetes (excessive thirst, frequent urination, fatigue, blurred vision), seek immediate medical attention.
